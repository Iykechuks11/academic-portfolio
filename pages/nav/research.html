Research Interests

My research focuses on the intersection of theoretical computer science, mathematics, and practical cybersecurity challenges. I am particularly interested in cryptography, information security, and the development of secure and efficient software systems.

Cryptography

Exploring both theoretical foundations and practical applications of cryptographic algorithms. This includes symmetric-key and asymmetric-key cryptography, hash functions, digital signatures, and emerging areas like post-quantum cryptography and lightweight cryptography for IoT devices. My work often involves security analysis and efficient implementation.

Related Projects

Information Security

Investigating techniques to protect data and systems from unauthorized access, use, disclosure, disruption, modification, or destruction. Topics include secure software development practices, vulnerability analysis, malware detection (as seen in my publication), network security protocols, and key management systems like Key Transparency.

Related Publications

Mathematical Foundations

Applying mathematical principles, particularly from number theory, abstract algebra, and information theory, to problems in computer science and cryptography. This includes analyzing the complexity of algorithms and understanding the theoretical limits of computation and security.

Related Notes

Secure Software Development

Focusing on building robust and secure software. This involves understanding common vulnerabilities (e.g., OWASP Top 10), applying secure coding principles, using static/dynamic analysis tools, and integrating security considerations throughout the software development lifecycle (SDLC). I also have experience with languages like C, C++, Rust, and Python.

Ongoing & Future Directions

Currently, I am deepening my work on lightweight cryptography analysis for my MSc thesis. Future interests include exploring the security implications of AI/ML systems, advancements in post-quantum cryptography readiness, and formal verification methods for security protocols.
